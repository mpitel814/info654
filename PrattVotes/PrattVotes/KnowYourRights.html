Know Your Rights

Know Your Rights at the Polls

Every voter has certain rights protected under federal and state law. Understanding these protections ensures that you can cast your ballot confidently and without interference. Below is a plain-language overview of the rights that matter most for New Yorkers.

1. Freedom From Discrimination

The Voting Rights Act protects you from discrimination based on race, color, or membership in a protected language minority group. No one at a polling place may treat you differently because of your identity or background.

2. Right to Assistance

You may bring someone with you to help you vote, as long as it is not your employer or union representative.

New York also provides:

  • Language assistance at select sites
  • Ballot marking devices for accessibility
  • ADA-compliant entrances at polling places

3. Right to a Provisional (Affidavit) Ballot

If you're told your name is not in the pollbook, you are still entitled to vote using an affidavit ballot. Do not leave without voting.

4. Protection from Intimidation

Intimidation is illegal. This includes aggressive questioning, blocking the entrance, threats, misinformation, or attempts to influence your vote.
